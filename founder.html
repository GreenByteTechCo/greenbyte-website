01 The Beginning

Halifa Rabiu Ibrahim — known as Khalifa Elgezy — grew up in Gezawa, Kano State, the son of a farmer. He watched technology transform the world on a screen while the world around him remained untouched by it.

That gap became his motivation. Not frustration — fuel.

He enrolled in Computer Science at Federal University Dutse, Jigawa State, and spent four years not just studying, but building — a series of increasingly ambitious projects that form the foundation of GreenByte Tech Co.

02 The Builder Mindset

Khalifa doesn't separate learning from building. His first Flutter app was for farmers. His first AI project used Hausa language. His first hackathon project — StudyLink FUD — served his own campus community.

He teaches digital skills on SkillMint Africa while simultaneously architecting blockchain systems. He designs UI in Figma, writes smart contracts in Solidity, and trains language models in Python. He is, by instinct and by choice, a full-stack founder.

04 The Vision

Khalifa's long-term ambition is not just to build a successful company — it's to build an institution. GreenByte Tech Co is the vehicle. The destination is a global technology company that proves, permanently, that Africa is not a market to be served — it is an engine of innovation.

He is also building toward a global NGO that uses technology for community impact — an extension of the values driving every product he has built so far.
